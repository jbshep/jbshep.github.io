Professional Experience

Bio

Dr. Shepherd joined the faculty at Buena Vista University in 2007. Prior to joining the faculty, he spent a decade working as a software engineer and IT architect, having spent most of his time in the national defense (DoD), energy, and transportation industries. He enjoys bringing those experiences into the classroom so that students can see the connection between computer science theory and real-world practical skills. Dr. Shepherd's research interests include user interface design and testing, algorithms, software engineering, and parallelization of interval methods.

Research and Projects

Overview

We try to foster a culture of curiosity and inquiry at BVU. One of the best ways to build real-world skills and master the concepts learned in class is by doing undergraduate projects and research. Our students often work on projects under the supervision of a faculty mentor, and ultimately present their work at BVU's Scholars Day.

Past Projects

Some of the areas students have done research in are:
  • Android and iOS mobile development
  • Web development
  • Video game enhancement (such as extending game maps, tweaking AI, and building controller support for keyboard/mouse-only games)
  • Robotics
  • Parallel computing
  • Linux system authentication
  • Scientific computing/computational science
  • "Dropbox-like" apps

Current Research Opportunities with Dr. Shepherd

The following are on-going projects within the BVU Computer Science department. Students are encouraged to talk to Dr. Shepherd to join the fun! :) Dr. Backman and Dr. Stone also have some interesting projects, and students are encouraged to talk to them as well.

Mobile app development

  • Description: Build iPad, iPhone, and Android apps to be deployed to the Apple App Store and Google Play. App ideas include games, campus information apps, communication apps, and learning apps. Thus far, we've had students build both native apps (iOS, Android) and cross-platform mobile apps (jQuery Mobile, PhoneGap).
  • Needed background: Students must have completed CMSC 181 Computer Science I. The following classes would be helpful, but are not required provided students are particularly well-disciplined and ambitious: CMSC 182 Computer Science II, CMSC 310 Algorithms and Data Structures, and CMSC 322 Web and Network Programming.

User-centered design

  • Description: Design and implement novel Web and mobile interfaces using user-centered design (UCD) methodology. The application we are building is a novel social learning management system.
  • Needed background: Students should have completed CMSC 181 Computer Science I at a bare minimum. CMSC 351 User Interface Design and Development would be helpful, but is not required.

Interval methods

  • Description: Use interval methods to bound and accelerate solutions to known NP-hard problems in computational science. Possible applications include robot motion planning, finding points of discontinuity, etc.
  • Needed background: Students should have as much programming and mathematics as possible, preferably CMSC 310 Algorithms and Data Structures, MATH 160 Calculus I, MATH 161 Calculus II, MATH 322 Linear Algebra, and MATH 371 Numerical Analysis.
